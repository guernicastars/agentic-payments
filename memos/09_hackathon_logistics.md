# 09 — Hackathon logistics

**Date:** 2026-04-25

## Schedule

- **Friday 7pm** — Reception, rooftop overlooking London skyline. Networking. Selected candidates only.
- **Saturday 10am – 8pm** — Full-day build, central London. 10 hours.

## Theme

AI agents that manage flow of money. Real-world problems: rent, invoicing, international payments, reconciliation.

## Prize

First place = guaranteed YC interview with **Tom Blomfield** (YC Partner, Monzo founder).

## Judging signal

Per Eugene's read: assessment is **founder potential via pitch**, not "best product". Pitch is **2 minutes**. Working demo is signal of velocity, not the artifact being judged.

## Implications

1. The pitch is the deliverable. Build supports the pitch.
2. Spend ≥1 hour rehearsing on Saturday afternoon.
3. Optimise for *one* killer slide (UMAP cluster plot with anomaly explanations) over a complete product.
4. Frame as "Bloomsbury Tech" (existing ML lab with publications) not "we're a couple of guys with an idea". Credibility compounds with Blomfield.
5. Skip vision; lead with the gap.

## Reception strategy

- Goal: pressure-test the pitch on 2-3 fintech-savvy people. Listen for the part that makes their face register confusion or skepticism — that's what to fix Saturday morning.
- Don't reveal the technical approach (UMAP, the polymarket asset reuse). Reveal the *gap* (human-baseline fraud blindness).
- Look for potential cofounders if the cofounder bandwidth is short.
