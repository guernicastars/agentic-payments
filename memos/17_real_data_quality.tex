\begin{abstract}
This memo documents quality issues observed in the Dune-pulled x402 snapshots,
records the decisions taken in response, and quantifies the cost of each
decision. The principal finding is that the 2025-10-15 snapshot is
\emph{bipartite-degenerate}: a single merchant captured 99.98\% of volume,
and the snapshot represents one institutional pilot rather than a
population-level distribution. We exclude it from the trend in memo~16 and
preserve it in the appendix as a regime-break signal.
\end{abstract}

\section{Oct 2025 snapshot -- bipartite-degenerate, excluded from trend}

The 2025-10-15 daily snapshot looked anomalous on first read (89 tx, mean
\$685.88, median \$108 versus \$0.10/\$0.01/\$0.001 on the rest of the trend).
A per-merchant breakdown in
\code{experiments/snapshot_validation.py:per_snapshot_summary()} produces:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule
Metric & Value \\
\midrule
Top-1 merchant volume share & 99.98\% \\
Top-1 merchant tx count & 85 of 89 (95\%) \\
Top-1 merchant address & \code{0x42ec2a5f...} \\
Total volume that day & \$61{,}043 \\
Volume to top-1 & \$61{,}032 \\
\code{n_merchants_to_50_pct} & 1 \\
Gap top-1 vs top-2 share & 0.9997 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

This is not whale-tx contamination -- it is \emph{whale-merchant
contamination}. A single contract was the destination of essentially all the
day's x402 traffic. Even after dropping the top five transactions, the median
stays at \$99.88 because the remaining 84 tx all go to the same merchant.

The full statistical comparison against the other three snapshots is in memo
16, hypothesis~H3. The qualitative summary: every snapshot has a single
dominant merchant, but Oct is the only one with no long tail -- the second
merchant adds 0.018\% rather than 10--25\%.

\subsection*{Decision}

Drop 2025-10-15 from the trend chart. The remaining three points
(Jun 2025 / Jan 2026 / Apr 2026) form a clean log-decay line on median tx
value: \$0.10 $\to$ \$0.01 $\to$ \$0.001.

\subsection*{How we frame it if asked at the pitch}

\begin{quote}
``October 2025 was bipartite-degenerate: 99.98\% of x402 volume that day
went through a single merchant address. We treat it as a transitional-phase
signal -- institutional pilots run before broad agent populations come
online -- not a trend point. The fact that we can detect this with a
30-second sanity check is part of what we're shipping.''
\end{quote}

This actually strengthens the narrative: it shows the system distinguishes
between population-level shifts and single-actor distortions, which is
exactly what fraud-vs-noise detection requires.

\section{Other quality notes}

\paragraph{Live x402 thinness.}
1{,}021 rows in a 60-min window $=$ $\approx 17$ tx/min average $\to$ 84k tx/day
extrapolated. Consistent with the 7-day facilitator query
($\approx 590$k tx / 7 days). Scale-consistent.

\paragraph{Baseline contamination.}
Baseline includes any non-x402 ETH transfer on Base. This will sweep in MEV
bots, DEX traders, and bridge users. Acceptable for the hackathon: it
produces a ``humans + non-x402 automation'' cluster, and x402 agents separate
distinctly. Production fix: filter baseline against known MEV bot lists
(flashbots, mev-inspect dataset).

\paragraph{Jun 2025 expanded to month-long window.}
1h-at-noon returned $\approx 0$ rows; expanded to the full month to obtain
151 transactions. Acceptable because Jun 2025 represents the
``early adopters'' phase explicitly, where 1h windows are not statistically
meaningful. The trend uses median tx, which is robust to the wider window.

\section{Ingest schema mapping}

The repository has three ingest paths feeding \code{compute_features()}.
\code{compute_features} reads only
\code{from_addr / to_addr / value_usd / block_time_s / gas_used /
gas_price_gwei / method_id / success}, so the divergence below is benign --
but worth knowing when joining tables.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lllll}
\toprule
Field & \code{synthetic.py} & \code{ingest/base.py} & \code{ingest/public_x402.py} & Snapshot parquets \\
\midrule
Payer wallet      & \code{from_addr} & \code{from_addr}$^\ast$ & \code{from_addr}$^\ast$ & \code{payer} \\
Recipient         & \code{to_addr}   & \code{to_addr}    & \code{to_addr}      & \code{merchant} \\
x402 flag         & \code{is_x402}   & \code{is_x402}    & \code{is_x402_eip3009} & file = snapshot \\
Facilitator       & \code{facilitator} & \code{facilitator} & \code{facilitator_addr} & n/a \\
USDC value        & \code{value_usd} & \code{value_usd}  & \code{value_usd}    & \code{value_usd} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\noindent$^\ast$ EIP-3009 signer, decoded from calldata; not the facilitator
that paid gas.

\code{src/viz/trend.py:_summarise()} handles the
\code{payer/merchant} $\leftrightarrow$ \code{from_addr/to_addr} rename
automatically.

\section{Reproducibility}

All raw data and execution\_ids are committed in
\code{data/raw/dune_results/summary.json}. Re-fetch costs zero datapoints via
\code{--use_cache}. The validation pipeline is one command:

\begin{lstlisting}
python -m experiments.snapshot_validation
\end{lstlisting}

This reads the four \code{data/raw/x402_snapshot_*.parquet} files,
re-computes every metric in this memo, regenerates
\code{results/figures/median_compression_bootstrap.png} and
\code{results/figures/concentration_comparison.png}, and writes
\code{results/tables/hypothesis_results.csv}.
